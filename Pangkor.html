Pulau Pangkor - Perak

The rugged landscape of Pangkor Island is still covered with lush vegetation. Because there are so many entertaining things to do on the island, it is well regarded as a very interesting tourist location to visit with the family. Pangkor Island is also a historical island because the Dutch once utilised it to defend themselves against invading forces. As a result, the island has a variety of fascinating historical sites, including the Dutch city, Batu Bersurat, and the Dutch Cemetery area. The word "Pang Ko," which in Thai means "beautiful island," is the source of the name Pangkor Island. Its moniker, which alludes to the island's attractiveness, may draw tourists. Another attraction is of course the beaches found here, each of which offers soul-soothing beauty. You can also rent a boat to visit other nearby islands such as Giam Island, Mentangor Island and Pangkor Laut Island.

Interesting activities that can do at Pulau Pangkor:

  • Water sport like banana bot, water ski and canu
  • Snorkeling
  • Island hopping
  • Jungle trekking
